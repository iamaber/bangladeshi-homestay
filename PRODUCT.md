# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International travellers — primarily from Europe (Switzerland, Germany, France, Italy) — seeking authentic cultural immersion rather than conventional tourism. They are curious, well-travelled, and value personal connection over convenience. They arrive on the site with a question: "What if I could really live somewhere, not just visit?" Their context is leisurely research on a laptop or large screen, often in the evening.

## Product Purpose

Bengal Living connects international guests with carefully selected Bangladeshi host families for a fully immersive two-week stay. The website is the product: it must make the intangible — trust, warmth, cultural immersion — tangible through editorial design, real photography, and honest storytelling. Success is a booking, but the deeper goal is that a visitor leaves the site feeling they already know the family they will stay with.

## Brand Personality

Bold, immersive, alive. Three words: **warm, genuine, vivid.**

The brand feels like a hand-written letter from a friend who wants you to see their world, not a brochure from a travel company. It should evoke the sensory richness of Bangladesh — colour, food, sound, human warmth — while maintaining editorial restraint. The tone is confident but never pushy; intimate but never unprofessional.

## Anti-references

- **Generic travel booking platforms** (Booking.com, Airbnb, TripAdvisor): cookie-cutter card grids, heavy filters, noisy UI, impersonal.
- **SaaS landing page templates**: purple gradients, oversized metrics, identical icon-heading-text card rows, glassmorphism, gradient text.
- **Heavy beige-only palettes** and overly decorative backgrounds that feel like a spa brochure.
- **The hero-metric template**: big number + small label + supporting stats + gradient accent. SaaS cliché.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Use real photography, family quotes, and sensory details rather than marketing adjectives. The family kitchen is more persuasive than a feature list.
2. **Trust through transparency.** Every price, inclusion, and step is visible and honest. No hidden fees, no dark patterns, no urgency pressure.
3. **Warmth through restraint.** Ample whitespace, quiet colours, and careful typography let the content breathe. Over-designing kills the emotional connection.
4. **Editorial rhythm over grid monotony.** Vary section pacing, typography scale, and layout density to create a reading experience that feels like a magazine, not a dashboard.
5. **Accessible to all.** WCAG 2.1 AAA compliance. Every guest should be able to navigate, understand, and book regardless of ability.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AAA target
- Semantic HTML throughout
- Keyboard-navigable accordions and menus
- Reduced-motion media query support
- Colour contrast checked for all text/background pairs
- Alt text for all images
- No autoplaying media
- Language-switcher clearly labelled
